## Structure

Hauls are carriage tasks which can be taken on by a fantasy merchant in a roleplaying game.

Each haul needs four authored fields. Destination, hint, payout, and delivery flavor are generated dynamically at runtime from the trade route graph.

**Fields to fill in:**
- **Name**: What the player sees in their inventory
- **Origin biome**: Which biome's settlements offer this haul (plains, mountains, forest, scrub, swamp)
- **Destination biome**: Which biome's settlements pay for this haul
- **Origin flavor**: 1 sentence — why this item is moving right now (shown when claiming)
- **Delivery flavor**: 1-3 sentences — what happens when you hand it over (shown on delivery)

### Packet of Hallucinogenic Mushrooms
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: mountains
- **Origin flavor**: A herbalist says she's been sitting on these since the forager brought them in last month; the apothecary up at the ridge put in the order half a season ago, and she'd rather not keep them in the shop.
- **Delivery flavor**: The apothecary unwraps the packet carefully and counts what's inside, then reseals it without comment. She pays you without looking up. From the back room, someone coughs and asks "Is that the order?" "Yes," she says. "Tomorrow." She hands you your coin.


### Boar Tusk Necklace
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: scrub
- **Origin flavor**: A hunter had it made as a gift but fell out badly with the man it was meant for; a gem trader passing through offered him half its value just to be rid of it.
- **Delivery flavor**: The clan elder examines each tusk, holding them up to the light. He turns the necklace over to inspect how the cord is knotted — a different method than he's used to. He nods, apparently satisfied, and pays. He puts it around his own neck.

### Bundle of Arrow Shafts
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: scrub
- **Origin flavor**: The fletcher's regular supplier has been sending short on orders for three seasons; someone finally arranged to have a proper bundle brought in from the forest towns.
- **Delivery flavor**: The fletcher counts the shafts one by one, checking each for straightness against the light. He sets eight aside immediately. "These are acceptable," he says, meaning the rest. He pays for them all, including the eight.

### Fox Pelt
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: scrub
- **Origin flavor**: A trapper writes that it arrived in good condition, timed well ahead of the cold season, for a furrier who placed the order in spring.
- **Delivery flavor**: The furrier lays it flat on her cutting table, strokes the fur against the grain, and lifts a corner to check the backing. She prods a spot near the shoulder where something healed. "This is fine work," she says, meaning the preparation, not the old wound. She folds it and pays.

### Cedar Oil Flask
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: scrub
- **Origin flavor**: A merchant who does the forest-mesa run says it's becoming harder to come by; the clan medic put in a standing request and this is the first flask in nearly two months.
- **Delivery flavor**: The medic uncorks it, brings it to her nose, and inhales slowly. She says nothing for a moment. Then: "Sit. Have some water. You look like you came through the pass in high wind." She tucks the flask into her cabinet and pays you from a purse kept behind the door.

### Birch Bark Scroll Case
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: scrub
- **Origin flavor**: A Kesharat registrar says he lost his in a flash flood three seasons ago and has been rolling his survey documents in oilcloth ever since, which is not ideal.
- **Delivery flavor**: The registrar removes the oilcloth from the scroll on his desk and slides it carefully into the birch case. It fits. He tests the cap. He slides the scroll back out, then in again. He is pleased in a quiet, administrative way. He pays you precisely and notes the transaction in his ledger.

### Forester's Marking Axe
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: scrub
- **Origin flavor**: A plateau woodwright ordered it through a contact in the forest towns; she needs to mark timber cuts in the terrace groves but says her regular axe is too heavy for the work.
- **Delivery flavor**: She inspects the marking face carefully and tests the edge with her thumb. Then she walks out the door and marks a post on the compound wall with three deliberate strokes. She studies them. She comes back in and pays.

### Dried Herb Bundle
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: A stilt-village healer says the swamp variety helps with the bite but the forest strain dries better and keeps longer; she's been asking for it specifically since last summer.
- **Delivery flavor**: She unbinds the bundle and holds a sprig up to the light, turning it. She pinches off a small piece and rubs it between her fingers, then presses her fingers to her wrist to smell it. "Good." She stores it in a sealed clay pot and pays you. She's already thinking about the next order before you've left.

### Woven Bark Mat
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: A craftsman says the reed mats rot out in a single season; his wife ordered the bark version two months ago after the third one went soft.
- **Delivery flavor**: His wife takes it and kneels on it immediately, right there in the doorway. She shifts her weight. She stands up and kneels again. She nods. "This one will last." He pays you and she carries it inside.

### Acorn Flour Sack
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: An Alwati baker says she used to make it herself before the nut trees were cleared from the village margins; now she can only get it when traders bring it in.
- **Delivery flavor**: She opens the sack and takes a pinch of flour, rubs it between her fingers and inspects it. She tastes a small amount. "A little coarser than I want," she says, but she's already weighing out payment. She'll use it. She always does.

### Impressive Deer Antlers
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: A revivalist elder says the antlers are needed for a ceremony she is reconstructing from partial temple carvings; the forest hunters produce a finer rack than anything taken from the swamp margins.
- **Delivery flavor**: She takes them from you with both hands and holds them for a moment before speaking. Then she carries them inside without a word. When she comes back out, she has the coin ready in her palm. "They're right," she says. You're not sure right for what. She doesn't explain.

### Wooden Bowl Set
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: A village carpenter says she can shape the bog-oak well enough but it dulls her tools; the forest-turned bowls are cheaper than replacing blades every season.
- **Delivery flavor**: She counts them and stacks them. She taps the largest against the table, listening to the tone. She turns one over to look at the base. "They'll do," she says, and pays. She keeps one on the table and puts the rest away.

### Root Dye Concentrate
- **Origin biome**: forest
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: A weaver says the swamp indigo is the best she has, but the forest red rounds out the palette and she's been substituting iron-rust, which is a poor comparison.
- **Delivery flavor**: She unstoppers it and holds it near the lamp, watching the color. She tips a drop onto a scrap of undyed cloth and watches it spread. She tips another drop. She pays you with the cloth still in her hand.

### 101 Kesharat Recipes
- **Origin biome**: scrub
- **Destination biome**: plains
- **Origin flavor**: A merchants' inn bought the book from a Kesharat functionary who needed road money; the innkeeper's wife has been trying to expand her menu since they added a second dining room.
- **Delivery flavor**: The innkeeper's wife opens to a random page and reads. She turns to another page. She finds something she recognizes and reads it carefully. "They use goat fat for everything," she observes. She closes the book, pays you, and opens it again before you've left.

### Bolt of Mesa Cotton
- **Origin biome**: scrub
- **Destination biome**: plains
- **Origin flavor**: A draper says the local wool is fine but his clients want lighter summer cloth; mesa cotton has been harder to come by since the rail started keeping it in Kesharat territory.
- **Delivery flavor**: He unrolls a yard of it and holds it up to the window. He weighs it in his hand, then stretches it lengthwise. He folds it back and runs his thumb along the selvage edge. "Thread count's decent." He makes a note of his supplier and pays you.

### Brass Tea Service
- **Origin biome**: scrub
- **Destination biome**: mountains
- **Origin flavor**: A Stift delegate says the tin service in his dormitory is an embarrassment; he ordered a replacement through a trader who knows the mesa routes.
- **Delivery flavor**: You deliver it to the dormitory corridor and a young delegate signs for it — clearly not the man who ordered it. Later someone brings payment to the gate. The next morning, walking through the Wandelgang, you hear the sound of a proper teapot through a door that is not quite closed.

### Clay Incense Burner
- **Origin biome**: scrub
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: A revivalist elder says the temple carvings show incense burning in the old rituals, and she can't reconstruct the ceremony without a proper vessel.
- **Delivery flavor**: She holds it in both hands and turns it slowly, comparing it to a rubbing she took from one of the carved reliefs. The shape isn't quite right. She sets it down and looks at the rubbing again. She pays you anyway and sets the burner on the altar she's been building.

### Sandstone Carving
- **Origin biome**: scrub
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: A young Alwati man bought it off a Kesharat trader and wants it as a gift for a family he owes a favor; he says the old folks will appreciate it, even if it's not their tradition.
- **Delivery flavor**: The elder unwraps it and sets it on the table. She studies it without touching it. She looks at the young man. "Where did you find this?" He explains. She looks at it again, then at the tilework on her wall, then back at the carving. She puts it away carefully. "Thank you," she says — to him, not to you.


### Signal Mirrors
- **Origin biome**: scrub
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: A trader says an Alwati fisherman put in a request after watching a Kesharat patrol use them; he thinks the mirrors could replace the drum signals between the outer villages.
- **Delivery flavor**: The fisherman unwraps one and holds it up, catching the light. He flashes it toward the house across the channel. Someone appears in the doorway, shades their eyes, then waves. He pays for both mirrors and practices the angles on the walk back to his boat.

### Sun-Dried Brick Mold
- **Origin biome**: scrub
- **Destination biome**: swamp
- **Origin flavor**: A carpenter says the stilt-village expansion is going to need more than plank and reed this time; he wants to try the mud-brick construction he saw in a Kesharat administrative town.
- **Delivery flavor**: He sets it on a table and measures it against his plans. He fills it with wet mud to test the release. The brick slides out clean and he examines it, then sets it in the sun. He pays you and looks at the mold again for a moment. "I'll need three more," he says.

### Certified Tile Specimen
- **Origin biome**: swamp
- **Destination biome**: plains
- **Origin flavor**: An imperial museum curator says a tile fragment matching the provenance she wants has finally surfaced; she's written to the trader twice about acquiring it and the sale has been agreed.
- **Delivery flavor**: She unwraps it and carries it directly to a display case, where she holds it against an existing panel. The pattern extends by two rows. She doesn't say anything for a long time. She pays from a discretionary fund and writes a letter by hand to someone who needs to know. She doesn't explain to you what the pattern means. You're not sure she knows.

### Treated Crocodile Hide
- **Origin biome**: swamp
- **Destination biome**: mountains
- **Origin flavor**: A mountain armorer says the swamp leather is the only thing that doesn't seize up in the cold the way treated cowhide does; she's had a special order waiting since spring.
- **Delivery flavor**: She rolls it flat on her workbench and measures it with a stick, marking two positions with chalk. She flexes the edge to test the treatment. "Whoever cured this knows what they're doing," she says. She pays and picks up her tools before you've left.

### Water Levels Report
- **Origin biome**: swamp
- **Destination biome**: mountains
- **Origin flavor**: A scholar says the flooding data from the outer swamp villages is the best long-term record available; she's been compiling it for a study on lowland drainage that four different institutions are waiting on.
- **Delivery flavor**: She takes it to her desk and opens it immediately, running her finger down the columns. She stops at something, makes a note, and keeps reading. "Tell whoever compiled this that the third-season figures are the ones I needed." She pays you without looking up. She is still reading.


### Swamp Willow Bark Strips
- **Origin biome**: swamp
- **Destination biome**: mountains
- **Origin flavor**: A mining-town healer says the willow bark from the forest is too dry by the time it arrives; the swamp variety keeps better and the ridge women swear by it.
- **Delivery flavor**: The healer puts a strip on her tongue without ceremony, then takes it off and examines the color. She bundles the lot and stores them in a waxed cloth pouch. "Good batch," she says, and pays. She's already calling through to the next room: "Ada, I have the bark."

### Jar of Sacred Mud
- **Origin biome**: swamp
- **Destination biome**: scrub
- **Origin flavor**: A Kesharat civil priest says it's for a comparative study; he was told the Alwati use it in ritual contexts and wants a sample from a reliable source.
- **Delivery flavor**: He opens the jar and smells it. He makes a note. He tastes the smallest possible amount on the tip of his finger and makes another note. He replaces the lid carefully. "You said this came directly from the temple district?" You confirm it. He pays and puts the jar in a locked case alongside eight others labeled in a script you can't read.

### Mangrove Root Wood
- **Origin biome**: swamp
- **Destination biome**: scrub
- **Origin flavor**: A plateau woodwright says the local scrub-wood is good but not dense enough for the tool handles she's been contracted to produce; the swamp timber is the right grain, if she can get it.
- **Delivery flavor**: She hefts each piece, feeling the weight. She makes a mark with a chisel on the end grain of the largest piece and examines the result. She runs her thumb across it. "This is good," she says, meaning it. She pays without negotiating, which is the highest compliment she has.
