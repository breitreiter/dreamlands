# Generate Haul Flavor: Deliveries to Forest

You are writing flavor text for trade hauls in a low-fantasy RPG. Each haul is a physical good carried by a player character from one biome to another.

## Rules

- **Origin flavor**: 1 sentence. Who wants this and why. Shown when the player claims the haul.
- **Delivery flavor**: 1-3 sentences. What happens when the player hands it over. Shown on delivery. Should feel like a small, grounded human interaction — no heroics, no melodrama. The recipient is a person with a specific need.
- **No place names, NPC names, or landmark names.** Use roles and descriptions instead ("a forest factor", "an exile camp trader").
- **Tone**: quiet, practical, lived-in. People are busy. Transactions are real. The world is not waiting for the player.

## Examples (filled entries delivering to forest)

### Bolt of Undyed Wool Cloth
- **Origin biome**: plains
- **Origin flavor**: A forest settlement ran short of cloth before the cold set in, and a factor placed an order with the nearest plains merchant before the roads muddied.
- **Delivery flavor**: The factor measures it twice against the bolt, nods, and counts coin into your palm. "Should last the winter. Maybe two." She folds one corner to check the weave and says nothing else.

### Depot Iron Mess Kit
- **Origin biome**: plains
- **Origin flavor**: A forest factor is collecting military surplus for resale to the deep-wood camps—the exiles can't get new ironware through normal channels, but they'll pay for something already made.
- **Delivery flavor**: The factor checks the rivet on the bail, tips water in to check for seep, and sets it in a crate with others. "Good condition," she says, and means it. She doesn't ask where you found it.
