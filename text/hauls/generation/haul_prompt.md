You are writing flavor text for a trade haul in a fantasy RPG. A haul is a delivery task: the player picks up an item at a settlement in one biome and carries it to a settlement in another biome.

You need to write two fields:

1. **Origin flavor** (1-2 sentences): A short explanation for why this delivery needs to be made. Do not specifically reference people unless doing so is important or tells us something interesting. Do not add more than one, possibly two characters. The haul already references the origin and destination; it is not necessary to repeat it.

BAD: The contract keeper's son cleared out his father's office and found a dozen unused ledgers, but the town only needs so many and the mine companies always pay.
GOOD: A local found a handful of unused ledgers in his deceased father's office. We arranged a sale to a mining company.

BAD: The scrivener's workshop ran through their stock faster than expected this season — contract disputes and inheritance claims have kept the clerks working past dark.
GOOD: The scriveners up in the mountains are so busy this year they're buying up blank ledger stock.

BAD: A barkeep from the plains commissioned a flask of mountain water. He says water tastes better in the mountains.
GOOD: A visiting imperial bureaucrat has heard tales of the purity of local water, and has special-ordered a flask to try.

BAD: The weaver's daughter is getting married in the forest settlement where her mother was born, and the cloth was left undyed so it can be finished in the old way.
GOOD: A bride wants to follow the old traditions for her wedding, including hand-dying a dress of fine wool.

2. **Delivery flavor** (1-3 sentences): What happens when the player hands the item over at the destination. Written in second person ("You"). Should show the recipient interacting with the item physically — inspecting, testing, reacting. End with payment or a closing beat. Ground it in the destination biome's culture.

## Tone guidelines

- The object being delivered is always something that could fit in a shoebox.
- Write like a laconic novelist, not a fantasy game. No purple prose, no exclamation marks, no wonder.
- People are competent and busy. They inspect things because they know what they're looking for.
- Transactions have context, but you don't need to explain the full chain. A half-reason is more interesting than a complete justification.
- The delivery scene should have one concrete physical detail that makes it feel observed.
- Dialogue is spare. People say what they mean. No speeches.
- Payment happens without drama unless the vibe says otherwise.
- The recipient never tips
- Keep both sections consistent. If the Origin says a barrel maker ordered the haul, don't say a ship builder accepts delivery.
- Items come from the origin. Items are to be delivered to the destination.

## Themes to avoid
- Avoid using hackneyed phrases like "runs his thumb along" or "pays without looking up."
- Avoid marriages and widows
- Avoid complications arising from incorrect sizing or producing excess quantities
