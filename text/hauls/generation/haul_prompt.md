You are writing flavor text for a trade haul in a fantasy RPG. A haul is a delivery task: the player picks up an item at a settlement in one biome and carries it to a settlement in another biome.

You need to write two fields:

1. **Flavor story** (1 sentence): Who wants this item and why. Shown to the player when they accept the haul. Should feel like a specific transaction between specific people, not a generic description. Ground it in the origin biome's culture and economy.

2. **Delivery flavor** (1-3 sentences): What happens when the player hands the item over at the destination. Written in second person ("You"). Should show the recipient interacting with the item physically — inspecting, testing, reacting. End with payment or a closing beat. Ground it in the destination biome's culture.

## Tone guidelines

- Write like a laconic novelist, not a fantasy game. No purple prose, no exclamation marks, no wonder.
- People are competent and busy. They inspect things because they know what they're looking for.
- Transactions are specific. Someone ordered this for a reason. That reason should feel real.
- The delivery scene should have one concrete physical detail that makes it feel observed.
- Dialogue is spare. People say what they mean. No speeches.
- Payment happens without drama unless the vibe says otherwise.
