# The Forester's Post (Forest Tier 2)

A well-maintained ranger station at the edge of exile territory. The ranger
who lives here has been the exiles' secret benefactor for years — misdirecting
search parties, leaving medicine at dead drops, falsifying reports. She's just
been recalled. A group of exiles has discovered that imperial soldiers are
coming to escort her back, and they've laid an ambush.

**Deeper concept:** Sustained individual compassion can keep a desperate
situation stable, and stability prevents self-sufficiency. The camps never
organized because one person's secret kindness made it unnecessary. Her
departure will be a crisis — and possibly the first real chance the exiles
have to become a community rather than a collection of dependents.

**Hook:** The PC comes upon a group of exiles crouched in the undergrowth
near a tidy ranger station. They say imperial soldiers are coming to arrest
their friend. They mean to kill the soldiers.

**Clutch:** The PC must navigate a situation where the exiles' loyalty, the
ranger's conscience, and the soldiers' orders are all pointed at each other.

# Scene Sketch

## The Ambush

The PC is moving through the forest when they nearly stumble into a group
of eight exiles lying in wait along a trail. They're armed with hatchets,
hunting bows, and one rusty imperial short sword. They are not soldiers.

Their leader is **Torben**, a wiry man with a missing ear. He pulls the PC
down and explains in a whisper: the ranger at the station up ahead,
**Warden Lise**, is the only reason half the exile camps have survived.
She's falsified patrol reports, left medicine caches, warned camps before
sweeps. Word came two days ago that she's been recalled to the capital.
Two soldiers are coming to escort her. Torben is certain "escort" means
"arrest" — you don't send soldiers to walk someone home.

Torben wants the PC's help. The exiles have numbers, but they're not
fighters. If the PC can get inside the station and open the back door when
the soldiers arrive, the ambush will be quick and clean.

## The Station

The PC approaches the station. It's orderly and well-kept — a single-room
timber building with a stone hearth, a desk covered in logbooks, and a
rack of dried herbs by the window. Maps on the walls. A cot in the corner,
neatly made.

**Warden Lise** is at the desk, packing. She's a tall woman in her fifties
with sun-darkened skin and steady hands. She's not surprised to see a
traveler — the station is on a trade path and she's used to visitors. She
offers the PC water and dried fruit.

If the PC mentions the exiles, Lise goes still. She knows about the
ambush — she spotted Torben's people this morning. She hasn't warned the
soldiers because they haven't arrived yet. She hasn't warned Torben off
because she's not sure he's wrong.

## Lise's Situation

Lise is honest with the PC if pressed. She's been covering for the exile
camps for eleven years. She doesn't consider herself heroic — she says the
camps are mostly families and displaced people who needed time to get on
their feet. Sending patrols after them was wasteful and cruel. She
falsified what she had to and let the forest do the rest.

The recall is genuine — not an arrest, as far as she knows. Her reports
have been increasingly inconsistent and her superiors want answers. She
intends to go, give her answers, and accept the consequences. She's tired.

But she's afraid of what happens when she leaves. The camps depend on her
warning system. Without it, the next ranger will run proper patrols and
the camps will be found within a season.

She asks the PC not to let Torben kill those soldiers. They're young. They
don't know anything about the camps. They're just here to walk her home.

## The Soldiers Arrive

Two imperial soldiers come up the trail in the late afternoon. They're
young, lightly armed, visibly bored. **Corporal Hadden** and **Private
Sera**. They greet Lise with casual respect and tell her they'll camp
here tonight and start back at dawn. They have no idea about the camps.
They are not a threat.

Torben's people are in the trees. The PC can see the glint of a bowstring
from the station window.

## The Clutch

### Help the Ambush

The PC opens the back door or signals Torben. The exiles rush the station.
It's brutal and fast. Corporal Hadden draws his sword and Torben puts a
hatchet in his shoulder. Private Sera drops her weapon and puts her hands
up.

Lise screams at Torben to stop. He does — Sera is alive, Hadden is
wounded but breathing. But it's done. The soldiers have seen the exiles,
seen the station, seen Lise's reaction. There's no version of this where
Lise goes back and everything is fine.

Torben tells Lise she can stay now. She doesn't have to leave. Lise looks
at the blood on her floor and says: "You've just made sure they send a
company next time, not a corporal." Torben says they'll deal with that
when it comes. The exiles have purchased time at a price they don't
understand yet.

### Negotiate

The PC talks to the soldiers and to Torben separately, trying to broker a
resolution. This requires convincing Torben to stand down and convincing the
soldiers to leave without Lise — or convincing Lise to slip away before
dawn while the soldiers sleep.

If the PC succeeds: Lise disappears into the forest. The soldiers wake to
an empty station and report that the warden deserted. Lise becomes an exile
herself — dependent on the same camps she protected, carrying the same
knowledge of the forest that made her valuable. Torben welcomes her. She
accepts without warmth. She knows what she's lost.

If the PC fails the persuasion: the situation collapses into the ambush
anyway, but messier. Torben panics and attacks before the PC can get clear.
The result is the same as helping the ambush, but with more injuries.

### Betray the Exiles

The PC warns the soldiers about the ambush. Hadden and Sera take defensive
positions in the station and Torben's people, seeing they've lost surprise,
scatter into the forest without a fight.

Lise leaves with the soldiers at dawn. She walks the trail without looking
back. The exile camps lose their protector. The next ranger will run the
patrols by the book.

Hadden thanks the PC sincerely. He says the PC probably saved lives — his,
Sera's, and the exiles who would have died if the fight had gone the other
way. He's right about that. He doesn't know about the camps that will be
found next season, or the families in them.
