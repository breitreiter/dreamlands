# Briar Commons (Forest Tier 2)

A half-finished commune in a hidden clearing, built by three exile camps who
tried to pool their labor and live together. It collapsed within a season —
not from violence, but from the realization that anyone who left the commune
knew where everyone else slept. The buildings are still standing. So are the
people, trapped by mutual knowledge, unable to leave and unable to trust.

**Deeper concept:** Community requires vulnerability, and vulnerability is
what exile exists to prevent. The commune didn't fail because the exiles were
bad people. It failed because the conditions of their freedom made
cooperation into a hostage situation.

**Hook:** The PC encounters a woman on the trail who introduces herself as
Dara, the village leader. She's returning from a long trip and invites the PC
to walk with her. She's optimistic, talkative, proud of what her people have
built together.

**Clutch:** After the PC witnesses the devastation, they must decide whether
to encourage or discourage Dara from attempting to start over.

# Scene Sketch

## The Trail

Dara is a solidly-built woman carrying a heavy pack. She's been to one of
the forest settlements to trade for seed stock and iron nails. She talks
freely about the Commons — three camps that decided to stop hiding from each
other and build something permanent. Shared gardens, a storehouse, a communal
kitchen. She's been gone two weeks and she's eager to get home.

She doesn't boast. She talks about the Commons the way a farmer talks about
a good field: with quiet satisfaction and practical detail. She mentions
specific people by name — **Oskar**, who built the storehouse frame,
**Maren**, who figured out the clay lining for the rain cistern, **Fen**,
who keeps the watch rotation fair. She's proud of them. She organized
this, and it worked.

## Arrival

The clearing is real and the buildings are real. Rough timber frames, a
half-shingled roof on the communal hall, garden plots marked with string.
It's the most organized settlement the PC has seen in the deep forest.

It's also nearly empty. A few people sit around a cold fire pit, not talking.
They look up when Dara arrives. Nobody greets her. Nobody moves to help with
her pack.

**Oskar** is sitting on the storehouse steps with an axe across his knees. He
nods at Dara. He doesn't stand.

## What Happened

Dara starts asking questions and the answers come reluctantly.

While she was gone, **Fen** left. Not dramatically — he packed his things one
night and walked into the forest. The problem was what happened next: the
remaining residents realized that Fen knew the location of the Commons, the
layout of every building, the sleeping arrangements of every person. He knew
where the food was stored and how many people were on watch at any given hour.

This was always true. But nobody thought about it until Fen left.

Then **Maren** pointed out that Fen also knew her real name and where she was
from. And Oskar's. And everyone's. The commune had been a place where exiles
stopped being anonymous — where they shared names and stories and histories,
because that's what community requires.

Now one of them is out there, carrying everything.

## The Collapse

Nobody accused Fen of anything. Nobody said he would betray them. But
within a week, two more people left. Each departure made the remaining
residents more afraid, because each person who left carried the same
knowledge. The departures accelerated. A few people started sleeping in the
forest instead of in their shelters, close enough to use the gardens but far
enough to run.

Oskar stayed. He stays because the storehouse has all the food, and someone
has to guard it. He's honest about this: he's not guarding it for the
community. He's guarding it because if he leaves, he has nothing, and if he
stays, he has walls and provisions. He has stopped pretending this is a
commune.

**Maren** is still here too, but she's dismantling the rain cistern she built.
She's taking her work apart piece by piece and stacking the components. She
won't say what she plans to do with them. When Dara asks, Maren says: "They're
mine. I built them."

## Dara's Response

Dara listens to all of this and is quiet for a long time. Then she starts
unpacking the seed stock and the nails. She lays them out on the communal
table as if nothing has changed.

She tells the PC that this has happened before. The first attempt at the
Commons failed the same way, and she rebuilt. She can rebuild again. Better
this time — she's learned that you need agreements about departure, rules
about what knowledge people can carry out. A system.

Oskar says a system is just another word for a prison. Maren says nothing.

## The Clutch

The PC must decide how to respond to Dara.

### Encourage Her

The PC tells Dara that the idea is sound and worth trying again. Dara is
visibly relieved. She has a plan: new agreements, a probationary period
for newcomers, a rule that anyone who leaves must go to a designated
settlement, not into the wild. She says she'll need the PC's help convincing
Oskar to stay.

Oskar listens. He doesn't say no. He says: "You're describing a place where
people need permission to leave." Dara says that's not what she means. But
it is what she described.

The PC leaves the Commons with Dara already drawing up new plans. The
buildings are still standing. Whether they'll be a commune or a
compound is a question Dara will have to answer without the PC's help.

### Discourage Her

The PC tells Dara, as gently or as bluntly as they choose, that the
Commons failed for a reason that can't be designed around. Exiles can't
build a community that requires trust, because the condition of exile is
the absence of trust. The commune didn't fail because of bad luck or bad
people. It failed because it asked people to be vulnerable in a place
where vulnerability is fatal.

Dara doesn't argue. She sits with the seed stock and the nails and says
nothing for a long time. Then she asks the PC to help her divide the
supplies evenly among whoever is left. She does this with the same
quiet competence she used to describe the commune.

The PC helps divide the supplies. Oskar takes his share and walks east.
Maren finishes dismantling the cistern and carries the pieces south. Dara
packs the nails — she paid for them, and she'll find a use — and asks the PC
which direction they're headed, so she can go a different way.
