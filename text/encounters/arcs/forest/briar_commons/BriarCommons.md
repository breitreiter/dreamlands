# Briar Commons (Forest Tier 2)

A half-finished commune in a hidden clearing, built by three exile camps who
tried to pool their labor and live together. It collapsed within a season —
not from violence, but from the realization that anyone who left the commune
knew where everyone else slept. The buildings are still standing. So are the
people, trapped by mutual knowledge, unable to leave and unable to trust.

**Deeper concept:** Community requires vulnerability, and vulnerability is
what exile exists to prevent. The commune didn't fail because the exiles were
bad people. It failed because the conditions of their freedom made
cooperation into a hostage situation.

**Hook:** The PC encounters a woman on the trail who introduces herself as
Dara, the village leader. She's returning from a long trip and invites the PC
to walk with her. She's optimistic, talkative, proud of what her people have
built together.

**Clutch:** After the PC witnesses the devastation, they must decide whether
to encourage or discourage Dara from attempting to start over.

Dara is a solidly-built woman carrying a heavy pack. She's been to one of
the forest settlements to trade for seed stock and iron nails. She talks
freely about the Commons — three camps that decided to stop hiding from each
other and build something permanent. Shared gardens, a storehouse, a communal
kitchen. She's been gone two weeks and she's eager to get home.

Choices:
- Ask about the commune: open Along the Way
- Carry in silence: open The Commons

## Along the Way
AlongTheWay.enc [bridge]

She doesn't boast. She talks about the Commons the way a farmer talks about
a good field: with quiet satisfaction and practical detail. She mentions
specific people by name — **Oskar**, who built the storehouse frame,
**Maren**, who figured out the clay lining for the rain cistern, **Fen**,
who keeps the watch rotation fair. She's proud of them. She organized
this, and it worked.

she contrasts this with other groups, who use code names and live in a suspended state of constant mistrust.

Choices:
- Continue to the commune: open The Commons

## The Commons

The clearing is real and the buildings are real. Rough timber frames, a
half-shingled roof on the communal hall, garden plots marked with string.
It's the most organized settlement the PC has seen in the deep forest.

It's also nearly empty. A few people sit around a cold fire pit, not talking.
They look up when Dara arrives. Nobody greets her. Nobody moves to help with
her pack.

**Oskar** is sitting on the storehouse steps with an axe across his knees. He
nods at Dara. He doesn't stand.

Dara starts asking questions and the answers come reluctantly.

While she was gone, **Fen** left. Not dramatically — he packed his things one
night and walked into the forest. The problem was what happened next: the
remaining residents realized that Fen knew the location of the Commons, the
layout of every building, the sleeping arrangements of every person. He knew
where the food was stored and how many people were on watch at any given hour.

This was always true. But nobody thought about it until Fen left.

Now one of them is out there, carrying everything.

Choices:
- Talk to Oskar: open Oskar's Plan
- Talk to Maren: open Maren's Plan

## Oskar's Plan
OskarsPlan.enc [bridge]
What happens: The PC engages with Oskar
Why does this scene exist: gives oskar space to unpack his case
What makes the scene grabby: oskar makes some good (if unpleasant) poins

Oskar is getting a group together to hunt down Fen and kill him. This seems a bit extreme, but oskar and his group reason:
- Fen is a danger
- He's broken a sacred bond of trust, he can't be rehabilitated
- He needs to be an example, or this will just happen again

He says Maren is telling people to leave, so his group needs to act boldly now. He thinks Dara is a good administrator but doesn't have the stomach to make hard decisions.

Choices:
- Talk to Maren: open Maren's Plan
- Talk to Dara: open Dara's Plan

## Maren's Plan
MarensPlan.enc [bridge]
What happens: Maren explains that she's planning to leave and encouraging others to do so as well
Why does this scene exist: we're building a case for the notion at the commons is fundamentally unworkable
What makes the scene grabby: gritty contrast

**Maren** is still here too, but she's dismantling the rain cistern she built.
She's taking her work apart piece by piece and stacking the components. she claims it's hers and she's taking it with her.

She's encouraging everyone to form smaller groups and leave. she knows of a safe place, but is evasive on the details. she thinks oskar and his group are insane. the thinks Dara's commune is a failed experiment and everyone is in danger.

She says she used to be a bandit before she fell in with Dara. She had real hope that there was someone with a good heart out here and that you could build a home. but it was a stupid thing to believe.

Choices:
- Talk to Oskar: open Oskar's Plan
- Talk to Dara: open Dara's Plan

## Dara's Plan
DarasPlan.enc [conflict]
How does the threat introduce itself: Dara is not engaging with the situation
Why can't the PC just walk away: they can, though that's also a choice
What levers does the PC have: picking a faction
What's at stake here: the commons

Dara is unpacking her purchases. She lays them out on the communal
table as if nothing has changed. Oskar and Maren walk in.

She tells them that this has happened before. The first attempt at the
Commons failed the same way, and she rebuilt. She can rebuild again. Better
this time — she's learned that you need agreements about departure, rules
about what knowledge people can carry out. A system.

Choices:
- Back Oskar
    - win negotiation
    Dara argues with you but ultimately agrees with Oskar's argument. the community needs to have laws to keep people safe. having laws means punishment for those who break them. Maren is terrified but silent. The place has become a prison for her and her people.
    - lose negotiation
    Dara exiles Oskar. After a tense moment, Oskar agrees to leave. Maren says she's still leaving.
- Back Maren
    oskar grouses but his group agrees to leave. Maren and her group return to packing. dara is crestfallen. she wonders if she's a poor leader or if living in anonymity and mistrust is the only option for forest exiles. Everyone parts ways and the commons is a failure.
- Back Dara
    - win bushcraft
    you tell dara about a place you ran across a few miles away. good soil, away from the roads, quiet. it will be hard work starting over, but if the community pulls together they can do it. new camp. new rules. new covenant. dara begins talking excitedly about her plans for new processes and rules. oskar and maren agree begrudgingly.
    - lose bushcraft
    Dara goes on about her big plans, but ultimately no one listens. the commons are compromised. oskar and maren's groups make plans to leave. dara stands on the common table and tries to make a rousing speech. a few people listen, then drift back to preparing to leave.
