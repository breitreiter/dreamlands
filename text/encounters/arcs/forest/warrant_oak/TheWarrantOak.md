# The Warrant Oak (Forest Tier 2)

A massive oak in a clearing where every exile who arrives nails up their
own wanted poster — a bounty notice, a warrant, a letter of disownment —
as proof they're "one of us." The tree is papered with decades of crimes
and accusations. It's become the exile zone's ritual of belonging: you
show what you're running from. 

**Deeper concept:** The forest exiles have a bad rep. Someone is using that to filter out dangerous folks.

**Hook:** The PC investigates the tree after hearing rumors about it. When they turn to leave, their path is blocked by a loose semi-circle of ruffians. They press the PC to confess to whatever crime brought them into the forest.

**Clutch:** The PC must decide whether to put themselves at risk to stop this group who preys on vulnerable folks mislead by false rumors. Or go along to get away.

# Scene Sketch

## Opening
Along your travels, you've heard the rumors of the Warrant Oak. Outcasts and exiles who flee to the forest must visit the great oak and post evidence of their crimes. The word is that if your crimes are impressive enough, a recruiter from one of the forest gangs will find you and invite you to join them.

You're not sure if you believe all that, but the tree in the clearing matches what you've heard.

## Examining the Tree
The tree is covered in warrants and confessions and wanted posters, just as you'd heard. But the actual crimes are less impressive. Most of these people are just from bad situations that they were unable to resolve any other way. Arranged marriages. Abandoned duties. People who have lost their religion or gained some new one.

If these crimes are meant to impress you with their heinous nature, most did not understand the assignment.

## Cornered
You turn to find a gang of rough-looking men in beaten copper masks. They report that they are "The Jury" and they're here to judge you for your crimes.

They're intentionally kind of ambiguous as to whether they're looking for impressive stories about your crimes or proof that you haven't committed any.

## Fight
If the player chooses to fight, it's a tough battle.

If the PC wins, the group scatters after one of their members falls. The dead ruffian has a sword and a letter destined for the closest scrub settlement. The letter is to their wife.

If the PC loses, they get beaten and robbed of their weapon.

## Claim to be a killer
The Jury wants nothing to do with you. They tell you that your type is not welcome in the forest.

## Claim to be a merchant
The Jury is unimpressed by your credentials, but they let you go unharmed.

## Lie and invent a story
The Jury have heard a lot of lies, so this is a tough test.

If the PC pulls it off, they weave the story to evoke the sympathy of the Jury. The ruffians are impressed, and welcome the PC as one of them.

If the PC fails, the Jury is offended and demand the PC leave.
