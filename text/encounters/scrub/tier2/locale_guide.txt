# The Occupied Mesa

The Kesharat Administration has pushed into clan territory. Rail lines cut across ancestral passes, depots sit on land that was clan-held for generations, and armed patrols walk the roads making a deliberate show of force. The comedy of the borderlands is gone—here the Kesharat are clearly an occupying force, justified by something they call "alignment with the Lattice." The clans resist through negotiation, obstruction, and violence.

Water is the flashpoint. Wells and springs that were clan property are now claimed by administrative decree. The rail brings laborers from elsewhere—people the clans don't recognize, working under conditions the clans find disturbing. Construction is always visible: new track, new signal towers, new depots pushing into contested ground.

The terrain is broken mesa country—plateaus, dry washes, boulder fields, dust. Wind is constant. Days are hot and exposed, nights cold. Navigation is by landmark, and the landmarks are sparse. The land produces spices and salt that both sides want, but the real contest is over infrastructure and control.

## Encounters

Territorial tension between clans and the Kesharat Administration drives most encounters. Water rights, passage through contested ground, rail construction encroaching on clan land. Not every encounter is hostile—some are transactional, some are territorial warnings—but nobody is relaxed. Hospitality still exists but it's conditional, political, offered by people deciding whether you're useful or a threat.

Encounters should feel tense, exposed, and jurisdictionally contested. Two kinds of authority overlap here: clan honor-law and Kesharat procedure. Neither recognizes the other.

## Scene Palette

Characters: a clan outrider watching from a ridge, deciding whether to challenge you, a Kesharat toll collector who is polite in a way that isn't reassuring, a herder whose well was claimed by administrative decree and who hasn't decided what to do about it, a rail construction overseer tracking progress on a clipboard, a clan elder negotiating passage terms with a face like carved stone, a merchant caught between a clan checkpoint and a Kesharat patrol, a civil priest explaining the Lattice to a hostile audience with evangelical patience, a water-seller with an armed escort and non-negotiable prices, a Kesharat deserter heading the wrong direction with nothing but the clothes on her back, a laborer on the rail line who won't make eye contact

Objects: a toll marker—a cairn with a painted stone meaning "you owe someone," a Kesharat census form in triplicate, a clan banner staked at a territorial boundary, rail spikes and graded earth where track is being laid, a confiscated weapon with an administrative tag, a water skin that's lighter than you'd like, a brass teapot battered enough to have crossed the barrens fifty times, a map scratched into a flat stone at a crossroads, a bundle of dried spice plants tied with cord, signal tower scaffolding half-built on a hilltop

Sensory: heat shimmer erasing the horizon, the crunch of mineral crust underfoot, wind that scours exposed skin, the rhythmic clang of rail construction carrying across the plateau, the absolute silence of midday when everything alive is hiding from the sun, the sudden cold when a cloud crosses the sun in high country, the taste of alkaline water, dust that works into everything, the low whistle of wind through a slot canyon, distant voices arguing in two languages

Activities: negotiating passage through territory that belongs to someone, sheltering from midday heat under an overhang, evaluating whether an approaching patrol is clan or Kesharat, rationing water with grim arithmetic, navigating by a landmark that doesn't match the description you were given, sitting through a hospitality ritual that is also a loyalty test, watching rail construction advance across land that was open range last season, wrapping cloth around your face against blowing dust, studying a scratched-stone map and disagreeing about what it means, listening to a civil priest explain the Lattice and feeling it start to make sense

Tone reference (do not reuse these scenarios): a clan checkpoint refuses passage because the Kesharat have claimed the road ahead, a civil priest offers to explain the Lattice and seems genuinely hurt when refused, a merchant's pack animal collapses from heat and he has to decide what to leave behind
