# The Corrupt Official
An authority figure — governor, magistrate, sheriff, judge — who weaponizes institutional power for personal enrichment or cruelty. Covers the full range from petty local tyrant to entrenched political machine.

# The Decadent Aristocrat
A nobleman or wealthy elite who treats cruelty as privilege and everyone beneath them as
property. Distinguished from the Corrupt Official by operating through status and impunity rather than formal authority.

# The Tyrant
A ruler or conqueror whose single-minded drive is dominance — over a city, a region, or an empire. Power is the point, not the means to one. May be coldly calculating or completely unhinged.

# The Military Butcher
A soldier or commander defined by willingness to commit atrocities. Could be an officer prosecuting a war of annihilation, a rogue who turned predator, or a deserter preying on civilians.

# The Warlord
A dethroned or exiled military leader who still commands loyalty and seeks restoration or revenge. Distinct from the Tyrant (who holds power) and the Military Butcher (defined by savagery) — the Warlord is defined by the campaign to reclaim what was lost.

# The Scheming Courtier
A chancellor, advisor, or insider who manipulates their way toward power — playing factions against each other, keeping hands clean, using proxies. Operates within institutions rather than against them.

# The Crime Lord
Runs an organized criminal operation — a city gang, a rural syndicate, a smuggling ring. Power comes from violence, fear, and corrupted institutions, not formal authority.

# The Corrupt Merchant
Profits from harm through commerce: arms dealing, wartime graft, debt bondage, poisoned goods. The market as a weapon. Distinguished from the Crime Lord by working through (outwardly) legitimate channels.

# The Hired Blade
A mercenary, assassin, or enforcer motivated by pay or the thrill of the work. No ideology, no long-term agenda — just a contract and the skills to fulfill it.

# The Zealot
Driven by fervent belief — religious, ideological, nationalist — that justifies atrocity in service of the cause. The true believer who has gone way too far (Knight Templar), as opposed to a hypocrite or fraud.

# The False Prophet
A cynical fraud who exploits religious or ideological fervor for personal gain — preaching one thing, practicing another. Distinct from the Zealot, who genuinely believes.

# The Puppet Master
Orchestrates events entirely from the shadows, using other people as pieces. Never gets their hands dirty. The plan is always several moves ahead.

# The Fallen Hero
Once genuinely good — now corrupted by grief, revenge, or loss. Their original values have curdled into something that causes the very harm they once fought. The tragedy is legible.

# The Well-Intentioned Extremist
Genuinely sympathetic goals, pursued through horrific means. They may be right about the problem and catastrophically wrong about the solution. Different from the Fallen Hero — this villain hasn't lost their values, they've followed them off a cliff.

# The Loyal Servant
Commits evil in faithful obedience — to a leader, a nation, an institution. Not malicious; the harm is incidental to the service. Defined by the inability or unwillingness to question.

# The Betrayer
Specifically a traitor in a trusted circle — a false friend, a double agent, a falsely-reformed villain who was lying the whole time. The wound is personal, not political.

# The Mad Professional
A physician, scholar, engineer, or artist who abandoned ethics for obsession. Does harm not from cruelty but from the total absence of moral restraint on their work. The craft is everything; the suffering is incidental.
