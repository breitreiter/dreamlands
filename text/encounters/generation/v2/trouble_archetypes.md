**Impassable terrain** — The route forward is blocked: washout, rockslide, fallen bridge, flooded crossing, sudden storm. The decision space is detour (costs time, unknown risk), improvise a solution (costs resources, tricky), or wait (costs time, random exposure). 
- pc vs environment

**Toll and extortion** — Someone with positional power (controls a bridge, a pass, a gate, a dock) demands payment, service, or compliance. The key structural feature is that they have *leverage but not necessarily legitimacy*. The spectrum runs from "this is an actual sanctioned toll" to "these are bandits with a chokepoint." 
- pc vs villain
- pc vs neutral

**Uncertain company** — You encounter a person or group and can't determine their intentions. The decision isn't about overcoming them but about *how much you expose yourself*: share a camp and risk theft, refuse and risk offense (or miss an opportunity), shadow them at a distance, alter your route to avoid. The tension is informational — you're making decisions under ambiguity, and the wrong read costs you differently depending on who they actually are.
- pc vs villain
- pc vs neutral

**Coerced labor or complicity** — Someone wants your help, but the ask is costly, dangerous, or ethically compromised. 
- pc vs neutral
- pc vs villain

**Suspicion and scrutiny** — You yourself become the problem. A settlement questions your presence, your goods, your story. Guards want to search your cargo. A local accuses you of being a spy, a smuggler, a bad omen. You haven't done anything wrong (or maybe you have), but you need to get *through* this social checkpoint
- pc vs neutral
- pc vs villain
