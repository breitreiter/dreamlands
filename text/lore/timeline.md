# Historical Timeline

Approximate dates are given in years before the present. The present is autumn of an unspecified year in the reign of Leopold III, reckoned by the Aldgate calendar.

---

## ~300 years ago — The Stift is founded

A splinter group of imperial academics, working in the high mountains, discover that certain judgments rendered under precise conditions and specific architectural geometries become metaphysically binding — words reshape reality to accommodate them. They build the original granite court complex on a peak, and over the following centuries a town of timber-framed dormitories, libraries, cafes, and administrative buildings grows around it. The Stift accumulates centuries of case law, its dockets stretch longer, and its bureaucracy calcifies into an institution that functions exactly as designed — with no mechanism for acknowledging its own failure.

## ~250 years ago — The Revathi are conquered

The empire pushes into the fertile lowlands and river valleys held by the Revathi, a thriving civilization with walled cities, intricate tilework, and a complex relationship with the swamp entity Revënakh. The conquest is catastrophic for the Revathi and a line item in an imperial ledger. The empire burns the cities after soldiers pry tilework off the walls. The Revathi are displaced into the swamp margins — land nobody else wanted, but which had long been part of their sacred geography.

Over the following centuries, the full language (Revathikh) decays into the clipped modern descendant (Revashu). Recipes are lost. Tilework arrangements can no longer be verified. The connective tissue of the culture disappears while fragments survive — blade-shapes, curse words, geometric patterns set into wooden walls in arrangements that different families insist are correct. By the present day, the loss is mostly finished. Elders carry inherited memory of the open conflict, but no one alive experienced it.

## ~150 years ago — The Kesharat destroy the Lattice-containers

The Kesharat, then a functional martial empire built on conquest, destroy the people who had been containing an extra-dimensional entity called the Lattice. The Lattice is unleashed and consumes Kesharat leadership — not physically, but as a parasite consumes will. Over the following generations, the empire ceases to be about martial conquest and becomes a bureaucratic machine serving the Lattice's drive to colonize and reshape the world. By the present day, the transformation is complete. Plateau clan elders remember their grandparents' stories of when the Kesharat were merely brutal but comprehensible enemies. The current Kesharat unsettles them in ways they struggle to articulate.

## ~60 years ago — The Grid campaign

The empire sends two legions (the 7th and 12th) into the far plains against the Grid — automated defense systems protecting an abandoned city. The legions are routed and decimated. The Grid forces stop short of Aldgate, which the empire reframes as a heroic stand ("The Defense of Aldgate"). The subsequent withdrawal ("The Retrenchment") leaves behind a skeleton of imperial infrastructure — forts, signal towers, supply depots, straight roads — all built for an advance that broke in weeks. The trees cut for siege timber have not grown back.

Veterans of the campaign are now elderly. Sev, an engineer in the Metal Beast arc, served as a boy runner during the fighting and is now in his seventies. The campaign is within living memory but only barely — the generation that fought it is dying, and what remains is myth, grievance, and salvage.

## ~30–40 years ago — Kesharat rail expansion

The Kesharat begin pushing rail lines into the borderlands between their territory and the plateau clans. The expansion is ongoing and accelerating. Rail construction cuts across clan-controlled passes and ancestral territory. Clan elders can personally remember life before the rails. The rail is both infrastructure and vector — it spreads the Lattice's influence into new lands.

## ~20 years ago — Imperial coinage reform

Old imperial coinage stops being minted, replaced by a new issue. The old coins still circulate in remote areas, particularly among people who distrust the current administration or simply haven't seen a new coin in years.

## Present day

Autumn, immediately before the harvest. The empire still rotates small military detachments to the wildlands each spring. The current platoons have been there half a year. Scavenger bands have entrenched in the abandoned campaign infrastructure. The Kesharat rail network continues to expand. The Stift's dockets stretch for decades. The Revathi sell tilework fragments to eat.
