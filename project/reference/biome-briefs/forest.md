# Forest — Trade Brief

## Culture
Working woodland society. Forest folk respect routine, distrust maps, mark trees obsessively, say little about the deep wood, and do not romanticize the forest. They endure it. Settlements are timber-framed cabins that blend into undergrowth — built to disappear, not to impress.

Deeper in: exile camps. Deep wood exiles are not villains; they are people who could not remain — deserters, debtors, lovers who ran, defectors from every biome. Loose camps, no flags, no shared banner beyond proximity. Everyone is hiding from something. They trade, they lie, and they keep watch.

The deepest forest is the domain of a solitary hunter — disciplined, patient, and competent. No one goes there voluntarily.

## Material Culture
- Weapons: woodsman's hatchet, hunting knives, clubs, boar spears, broad falchions, bows, staves, slings.
- Armor: hunter's leathers, bark-backed jerkins. Light and practical.
- Foods: preserved game, river fish, nuts, hardy carbs. Venison jerky, smoked trout, acorn bread, wild berry preserve, hazelnut paste. Foraged: squirrel, brook minnows, wood grubs, wild onion, blackberries, maple sap.

## Trade Profile
- Produces: resin/pitch, charcoal, tanned hides, figured wood, dye lichens, medicinal extracts, rare mushrooms, handcrafts (luck charms, carvings, feathers).
- Hungers for: metal tools, spices from the mesas, grain, cloth, ironware, anything the exiles can't get through normal channels.

## People & Tone
- Forest folk: short, consonant-heavy names (Bram, Edda, Korrin, Soren). Drab, functional clothing. Proverbs: "Cut what you carry." "Paths change."
- Exiles: mixed naming from every biome. Mismatched clothing. No pattern — that's the tell.
- Voice: laconic, practical, not sentimental. Deep distrust of outsiders in tier 2.
