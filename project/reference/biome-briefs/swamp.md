# Swamp — Trade Brief

## Culture
Lowland bog inhabited by the Revathi — a displaced people living in the land nobody else wanted. They once held fertile lowlands, river valleys, and walled cities with intricate tilework. Now they live in stilt-villages in the swamp margins. The loss is mostly finished; what remains is the quiet aftermath.

The Revathi are not in active crisis. They are practical, slightly melancholy, and divided between preserving what they have and selling it to survive. Their tilework fragments — gorgeous geometric ceramics salvaged from the old cities — are set into wooden walls in arrangements no one can verify are correct. Some face outward as decoration; others are used as cutting boards.

The empire doesn't garrison the swamp. It touches the edges: traders who come for "authentic" goods, census-takers who count heads, the occasional collector. The imperial presence is commercial and administrative, not military.

Deeper in: the old temple complex on clay ridges, half-sunken into peat. Bioluminescent fungi and moss. Young Revathi revivalists trying to reclaim heritage. A swamp witch who may not be a person. The deepest swamp is an organism that archives without comprehension.

## Material Culture
- Weapons: beautiful knives with wrong proportions (descended from spearheads), fishing harpoons, reed-shafted javelins, bog-oak clubs.
- Armor: almost nonexistent. Layered reed-weave for protection. Metal rusts, leather rots, anything heavy sinks.
- Foods: eel is the foundation — smoked, stewed, dried, fermented into paste. Frog, turtle, crawfish, freshwater fish. Tubers, wild ginger, bog cotton seeds, rice. Imperial spices used in feast recipes because the originals were lost.

## Trade Profile
- Produces: peat (fuel), smoked eel, reed-weave textiles, medicinal herbs (fever-bark, wound-moss), bog-oak timber, swamp dyes (deep indigo, ochre), leeches.
- Hungers for: metal (tools, nails, blade-stock), grain, salt, woven cotton or wool, luxuries that make swamp life bearable.
- Contested: tilework fragments and bog-preserved artifacts — enormous cultural value to the Revathi, enormous market value to imperial collectors.

## People & Tone
- Revathi: open syllables, vowel-heavy, soft consonants (Orun, Nalith, Kesra, Halisi). Loose-fitting cloth in muted dyes. Bare feet or reed sandals.
- Voice: quiet, practical, understated grief. People speak at the volume the swamp teaches, which is low. Not sentimental about their situation — they live with it.
