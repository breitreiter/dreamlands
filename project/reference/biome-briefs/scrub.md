# Scrub (High Mesa Corridor) — Trade Brief

## Culture
Wind-cut plateaus and dry riverbeds where honor-based clan politics collide with the Kesharat Administration's procedural bureaucracy.

**Plateau clans**: Highland kin-groups controlling passes, wells, and terraced valleys. Honor-based kinship with strong local autonomy. Hospitality is binding but political. Afghan highland-coded. They trade goat products, rare spices, handcrafted metalwork, salt from the flats. Reputation matters enormously — merchants who respect protocol trade freely.

**Kesharat Administration**: Descendants of a fallen empire, now civil stewards of infrastructure, rail, census, and quotas. Their leadership has been compromised by the Lattice — an extra-dimensional intelligence that colonizes minds and reshapes institutions. Lower-ranking personnel are bureaucrats who follow procedure with unshakable certainty. They value efficiency, standardized weights and measures, and paperwork. Rail lines cut across ancestral territory. In tier 1, Kesharat personnel are hapless fish-out-of-water. In tier 2, they're a serious occupying force. In tier 3, the pretense drops.

## Material Culture
- Weapons: jambiya (dagger), scimitars, desert lances, slings, walking staves. Fine weapons are family heirlooms.
- Armor: desert robes, sand-leather wraps, lamellar cuirass, mirror armor.
- Foods: goat jerky, smoked lizard, spiced locusts, flatbread, millet cake, date paste, fig leather. Foraging is difficult.

## Trade Profile
- Clan territories produce: rare spices, preserved foods, salt, goat products, handcrafted metalwork.
- Kesharat territories produce: sophisticated alloys, medicines, standardized industrial goods, silks.
- Hungers for: spicewood and timber (treeless plateaus), quality iron tools and weapons, exotic foods, mercenary labor.

## People & Tone
- Clans: hard consonants, compact names (Zarik, Davun, Haris). Layered robes in earth tones. Status through quality of weave and metalwork.
- Kesharat: balanced syllables, vowel harmony (Qiren, Meilin, Halren). Clean standardized clothing. Titles matter. Bureaucratic and impersonal.
- Voice: clan folk are direct, hospitality-driven, reputation-conscious. Kesharat folk are procedural, form-filing, eerily certain.
