# Plains — Trade Brief

## Culture
Agrarian heartland society built on contract law, grain trade, and civic duty. People believe in trade, incremental improvement, and stability. Conflicts are small-scale: merchant leverage, grain weights, inheritance disputes. No one doubts the system; they argue about their position in it.

Beyond the heartland lies the failed campaign zone — skeletonized imperial infrastructure, half-collapsed garrisons, scavenger bands salvaging military surplus. No towns, only outposts. Everyone is defensive. Scavengers operate like guilds, not marauders. Military patrols hold some forts and insist you shouldn't be here.

## Material Culture
- Weapons: tools that double as weapons — billhooks, staves, knives. Wildlands: military issue, faded and salvaged.
- Armor: leather, quilted jack. Guards might wear brigandine or mail. Scavengers wear mismatched salvage.
- Foods: grain, bread, porridge, beer, preserved meat, cheese, root vegetables, pulses. Wildlands provisions are army rations or scavenger fare from abandoned depots.

## Trade Profile
- Produces: grain, flour, wool, leather, beer, preserved meat, draft animals, seasonal labor. Scavengers produce military salvage.
- Hungers for: metal (tools, nails, weapons from mountain forges), salt, luxuries from other biomes, medicine, warm gear, information.

## People & Tone
- Heartland: archaic British naming. Warm wind, golden fields, wooden fences, church bells, ledgers and ink.
- Frontier: half-official naming. Brittle, defensive. Skeletonized infrastructure, watchfires never relit.
- Voice: polite, receipt-keeping, orderly. Frontier folk are blunt and suspicious.
