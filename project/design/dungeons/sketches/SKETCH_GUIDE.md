# Writing Sketches

The seed in the seed document is just that; a seed. It has some evocative concepts and a general skeleton. However:
- As we expand it and fill in details, we may choose to emphasize some elements and quiet others
- We may choose to change our perspective, looking at the situation from some new angle. Perhaps we're looking at it after the fact. Perhaps we're looking at a situation which will lead to what's described in the seed.
- We may decide to introduce new details that contradict what's in the seed. That's fine. What matters is that our work product, the sketch, is consistent.

When designing a dungeon, keep in mind:
- These are premiere experiences. We should generate several branching scenes during which the player learns more about the situation and makes decisions.
- There should be a final, irrevocable decision at the end. The choice should be ambiguous and difficult. It should tell us something about who the PC is.

Common mistakes:
- We can't specify how the PC comes to the dungeon, so we can't include any references to "you're delivering a package" or "a companion has been captured." We can only assume the PC is there now, on their own power, for their own reasons.
- The entire dungeon must be resolved on-site. The PC cannot promise to deliver something to another location or meet someone somewhere else. 
- The entire situation must be resolved in a few hours time. The PC cannot wait several days or come back later.
