
# PLAINS

## Tier 1 — Heartland

**Blank Ledger Book**
They say a counting house near the south gate has burned through three clerks this season and is buying up anything with clean pages and stiff covers. Someone’s preparing to count something big.

**Bolt of Undyed Wool Cloth**
The dyer on Tallow Street is buying cloth again. She refuses colored stock; wants to set the color herself, start to finish.

**Stamped Grain Weights**
Word is a miller was fined for “creative measures.” Now certain buyers are quietly collecting properly marked brass weights to avoid further embarrassment.

**Silver Trade Bar**
A courier house is offering a finder's fee for certified metal. Not coin, not ore, something a bank will accept without argument.

---

## Tier 2 — Wildlands

**Army Surplus Spearheads**
Some locals are keen to start a militia. They've been training with wooden staves, but would pay to turn those to spears.

**Salvaged Signal Lantern**
There’s talk of a caravan running night routes again. They’re paying for anything with shutters and a chimney, dents be damned.

**Cracked Surveyor’s Transit**
A land agent is mapping boundaries no one agrees on. He’ll take flawed instruments if the screws still turn true.

**Vitrified Stone Shards**
A collector in the market quarter is paying above rate for anything that came out of the dead zones. Says the glass-smooth ones are what he wants specifically. Pays for the story as much as the stone.

**Depot Iron Mess Kit**
A quartermaster turned tavern keeper is buying durable field tins in bulk. Claims they stack well and survive rough patrons.

**Collapsed Tower Bell Fragment**
The bell-caster near the south gate is looking for reference material. She wants a piece of the old work — says modern foundries have forgotten something.

**Imperial Road Marker Plaque**
A historian who drinks at the River's End keeps asking travelers if they've seen any of the old iron signage. Claims he'll pay honest coin, not trade.

---

## Tier 3 — The Abandoned City

**Grid Regulator Plate**
A woman from out of town has been asking around about machine parts, specifically the brass-and-ceramic kind with serial markings. She won't say what for.

**Untouched Market Coin Chest**
Rumor claims a private vault is buying ancient coinage. Not just the foreign stuff, like real antiques.

**Golem Memory Cylinder**
A workshop on the upper tier posted a notice asking for "thinking components." They're being discreet about it, but word is they'll pay extraordinarily well.

---

# MOUNTAIN

## Tier 1 — Mining Folk

**Ridge Salt Slab**
A provisioner for the lower passes is stockpiling salt bricks. Says caravans lick through more than you’d think.

**Charcoal Bundle**
A forge on the ridge is buying coal by the sack. Needs heat that burns steady and hot.

**Company Scrip Stack**
A labor organizer is offering real coin for scrip. Says she's building a case and needs a full accounting of what's in circulation.

**Raw Silver Ore Chunk**
The assay office is offering a small premium for reference samples. Sounds official enough, but the clerk's been making side purchases on his own too.

---

## Tier 2 — Scholars

**Annotated Legal Codex**
A barrister up from the lowlands is looking for anything with scholarly notes on inheritance law. Says he'll pay per page of useful marginalia.

**Precision Astrolabe**
An observatory assistant is seeking atronomical equipment. Says even older models will do if the calibrations remain honest.

**Printed Reform Pamphlets**
A bookseller with nervous eyes is collecting political essays. Claims there’s renewed interest in patient, circular arguments.

**Sealed Research Dossier**
An institution is recalling wax-stamped folios bearing its crest. Prefers them unopened; curiosity lowers the price.

**Engineered Retort Glassware**
An alchemical supply house is paying for precisely blown bulbs and tubes, provided the joints still fit snug.

**Corrected Mine Safety Blueprint**
A consortium of investors is buying revised technical drawings. Apparently caution is fashionable again.

**Observatory Lens Disc**
A patron of the sciences seeks flawless crystal discs, thick and cold. Will pay well for clarity at the center.

---

## Tier 3 — The Stift

**Filed Petition (Year 73)**
 A land claimant has been searching for decades for an original filing from Year 73. He says the paper trail exists, it just needs to be found.

**Binding Judgment Writ**
A family trying to contest an old ruling says the only way to challenge it is to produce the original decree first. Their solicitor will compensate a finder.

**Wegtafel Fragment**
A stonecutter has a commission to restore a roadside shrine. She needs old reference material, specifically pieces of the original carved directional work.

# FOREST

## Tier 1 — Forest Folk

**Stacked Firewood Bundle**
A roadside inn is buying evenly cut oak lengths by the armful. Winter’s closer than it looks.

**Tanned Deerhide**
A cobbler is paying for supple, smoke-cured skins free of rot. Says good grain is hard to come by this season.

**Charcoal Sack**
A silversmith says the fuel she's been getting from the lowland traders burns too fast and dirty. She's looking for the good stuff from old-growth burners.

**Pitch Resin Pot**
A boat builder at the river docks is caulking a new hull and ran short of compound. He needs the thick, workable kind, not the dried stuff.

---

## Tier 2 — Deep Wood Exiles

**Ghostcap Extract Vials**
A physician with a questionable reputation is buying certain tinctures through an intermediary. She's careful about what she asks for directly.

**Figured Heartwood Planks**
A master luthier is paying for boards with rare rippling grain, no longer than a forearm. Says the wood sings if cut right.

**Luck Charms**
A gambling house owner says his tables have run cold for a month. He's buying every ward and token he can find, no matter where they come from.

**Dryad's Knot Fungus**
An alchemist has been sending a runner to the market every few days asking about dried fungus. Seems anxious to acquire a quantity.

**Trail Kit Roll**
A guide company is buying compact leather rolls filled with blaze tools and treated twine. Expansion into deeper woods, they say.

**Unusual Local Writings**
A scholar of folk traditions is offering a standing fee for any personal journals or unusual writings coming out of the isolated woodland communities.

**Caged Songbird**
A wealthy merchant's wife fell in love with a strange bird she heard at a menagerie. Her husband is now offering a reward for one to be delivered to the house.

---

## Tier 3 — The Hunter

**Unsettling Wooden Doll**
A collector of curious objects offers good coin for "cursed objects" taken from the forbidden places in the deep woods. What he does with such things is his own business.

**Imperial Standard**
A noble family is quietly trying to recover historical insignia connected to their lineage. They say their grandfather led an imperial expedition into the interior.

**Master Snare Mechanism**
A gamekeeper on a private estate says his current equipment is embarrassingly loud. He wants something precision-built, from someone who actually understands the craft.

---

# SCRUB

## Tier 1 — Plateau Clans

**Caravan Spice Pouch**
A restaurant owner in the market district says her spice supplier went silent. She's asking every traveler if they brought anything with them.

**Hammered Copper Armbands**
A jewelry trader says clan-worked copper sells faster than she can stock it. She's buying whatever comes down the trade road.

**Perfumed Resin Cakes**
A bathhouse proprietor in the upper district says his imported scenting stock ran out two months ago and the clients have started to notice.

**Exquisite Kaftan**
A tailor is studying plateau construction techniques and will pay well for original garments. Not copies, not local interpretations; originals.

---

## Tier 2 — Kesharat Administration

**Rail Line Survey**
An independent shipping consortium is trying to plan routes without going through official Kesharat channels. They need documentation.

**Jade Slab**
A sculptor has a commission from a wealthy patron who requested any exotic material. He's been trying to source something for months.

**Signal Tower Clockwork Core**
An engineer who left Kesharat employment says she knows how the system works and just needs a unit to study. She's offering more than it's technically worth.

**The Lattice, An Introduction**
A merchant who does business in Kesharat territory is tired of not understanding what his partners are talking about. He wants a foundational text.

**Stamped Rail Spikes**
A blacksmith says a foreman brought him a broken Kesharat spike and asked him to reproduce it. He gave up and now he's simply trying to buy originals.

**Kesharat Robe**
A local clothier can’t get enough of heavy wool garments with bold geometric patterns. The more unmistakably foreign, the better.

**Confiscated Clan Banner**
A clan elder who fled the plateau is trying to recover symbols of his lineage. He doesn't advertise this, but those who know him say he'll pay whatever he has.

**Unusual Coins**
A numismatist's agent is in town buying anything strange for her employer's collection. She's paying three times face value and doesn't care if it's legal tender anywhere.

---

## Tier 3 — The Lattice

**Alignment Rod**
An engineer turned hermit is doing something in his workshop he won't describe. He's sent word that he needs one of the Lattice instruments and he clearly knows what he's asking for.

**Colorless Crystal Node**
A researcher studying optical phenomena has heard rumors of a material that bends light without coloring it. She's offering considerable resources just to examine one sample.

**Worker Identification Band**
A legal advocate documenting labor conditions under the Lattice wants physical evidence. She says a single band is worth more to her case than a hundred witness statements.

# SWAMP

## Tier 1 — Wetland Towns

**Bundled River Reeds**
A basket-weaver collective in the upland market says their local supply was ruined by an early frost. They're buying anything properly dried and trimmed.

**Peat Fuel Brick**
A smokehouse operator says peat burns slower and cleaner than what he's been using. He'll take as much as you can carry.

**Leech Jar**
The physician's assistant at the waystation has been out of stock on leeches for two weeks. The doctor won't say why he needs so many, but he's paying a premium.

**Resin-Sealed Waterproof Satchel**
A courier running the river routes says he's ruined his third bag this season. He needs something that can actually survive a capsize.

---

## Tier 2 — Specialists & River Traders

**Embalming Salts**
The undertaker received a contract for a long transit interment and says their supplier delivered short. They're buying through private channels.

**Blackwater Dye Vial**
A dyer says a noble house wants livery in a specific black she can't achieve with standard materials. She's been hunting for the true source color for months.

**Venom Ampoules**
A researcher is being very careful about how she phrases her interest, but she's been asking about preserved biological samples from the swamps. Something about needing to be "potent."

**Bog-Iron Ingot**
A weapon-smith says marsh iron works differently under the hammer and he wants to test it properly. He'll buy whatever's available.

**Preserved Lotus Resin**
A temple officiant says a certain resins have become difficult to source since the new trade road bypassed the marsh. They need them for the seasonal ceremonies.

**Witchfire Lamp**
A hostess at an exclusive gathering venue heard about "swamp lanterns" through a guest. She's made inquiries; very discreet, very persistent.

**Nightbloom Poison Resin**
A shady character has been complaining about a "rat problem." Says she need a very specific poison the swamp folk make from flowers.

---

## Tier 3 — The Swamp Itself

**Marsh Pearl**
A jeweler who works exclusively in unusual materials has been looking for truly unique gemstones. Her agent is currently in the region.

**Root Head**
A cabinet of oddities is paying for human-like root formations. No questions asked.
